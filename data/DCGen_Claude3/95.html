Making the Web work

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) develops standards and guidelines to help everyone build a web based on the principles of accessibility, internationalization, privacy and security.

Working with stakeholders of the Web

A range of organizations join the World Wide Web Consortium as Members to work with us to shape the direction of core web technologies and exchange ideas with industry and research leaders. We rotate regularly a few of our Member organizations' logos.

Web standards

Web standards are the building blocks of a consistent globally accessible world. They are implemented in browsers, tools, web servers, and many other places to create an interoperable World Wide Web.

W3C is an international community where decision-, full-time staff, and the public work together to develop web standards.

Get involved

W3C works at the nexus of core technologies, industry needs, and societal goals. Everyone can get involved with the work we do.

There are many ways individuals and organizations can participate in the Web Consortium to advance web standardization.
